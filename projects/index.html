    Projects

    CTRC Projects are hands-on builds that take the foundation skills you've learned and apply them to real, tangible creations. These aren't worksheets or simulations — you're designing, building, and testing physical machines.

    Philosophy

    The V5 Foundation Program teaches you how to build robots. Projects teach you why it matters. Each project is a complete engineering challenge: you start with requirements, design a solution, fabricate it, wire it up, and put it to the test.

    Projects are where you stop following instructions and start making decisions.

    Available Projects

    • BattleBots

      Design, build, and fight a ~500g combat robot with spinning weapons. 3D printed, brushless-powered, radio-controlled carnage.

    Suggest a Project

    Have an idea for a project? Talk to a mentor or submit a suggestion through the club. The best projects come from students who want to build something cool.

    Prerequisites

    All projects assume you have completed at least Unit 1 (Drivetrain Mastery) of the V5 Foundation Program. Some projects may require additional units — check each project's overview page for specific prerequisites.

    You should be comfortable with:

    • [x] Basic mechanical assembly
    • [x] Using hand tools safely
    • [x] Reading wiring diagrams
    • [x] Basic Onshape CAD skills

    How Projects Work

    Phase What You Do What You Deliver
    Research Study the rules, understand the constraints Requirements document
    Design CAD your solution in Onshape Reviewed 3D model
    Fabricate 3D print, cut, and prepare parts Physical components
    Assemble Wire, mount, and integrate everything Working machine
    Test Verify it works, iterate on problems Tested and tuned build
    Compete Put it to the test against others Battle scars and lessons learned

    Document Everything

    Take photos at every stage. Keep notes on what worked and what didn't. The best engineers learn as much from failures as from successes — but only if they remember what happened.
