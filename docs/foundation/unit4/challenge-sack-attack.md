# Challenge: Sack Attack

**Grab everything. Score everything. Sixty seconds.** Sack Attack tests your intake mechanism under time pressure. Bean bags are scattered across the field, and your robot must intake as many as possible in one minute. Every tuning decision you made in Module 4.2 — compression, deadzone, roller speed — shows up here.

---

## Objective

Intake and hold or score as many bean bags as possible in 60 seconds using your top roller intake mechanism from Module 4.2.

---

## Format

| | Details |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Individual, timed run |
| **Duration** | 60 seconds per run |
| **Attempts** | 2 runs per student, best score counts |
| **Mechanism Required** | Intake from Module 4.2 |
| **Game Objects** | 10+ bean bags (or substitute soft game objects) |

---

## Field Setup

- **Field size**: Standard VEX field or a taped-off 8' x 8' area
- **Bean bags**: 10-15 bean bags scattered randomly across the field
- **Starting position**: Robot starts in a corner, intake facing the field
- **Scoring zone**: A designated area (box or tray) where intaked bean bags must be deposited

## Rules

### Setup
1. Robot starts in the designated corner with intake facing the field
2. Bean bags are placed by the coach in random positions
3. Driver holds the controller and waits for the start signal
4. Timer is visible to the driver

### During the Run
1. Coach says **"Go"** — 60 second timer starts
2. Driver controls the robot to intake bean bags
3. Robot must **intake the bean bag** (pull it into the robot using the intake mechanism) — pushing or bulldozing does not count

!!! example "📷 Photo Needed"
    Robot intaking a bean bag during a timed run
4. Intaked bean bags must be **deposited in the scoring zone** to earn points
5. Bean bags that fall out of the robot before reaching the scoring zone do not count
6. If a bean bag is only partially intaked (hanging out of the robot), it does not count unless it reaches the scoring zone

### Scoring

| Action | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Bean bag successfully intaked AND deposited in scoring zone | **1 point** |
| Bean bag intaked but dropped before scoring zone | **0 points** |
| Bean bag pushed into scoring zone (not intaked) | **0 points** |

### End of Run
1. Timer reaches 0:00 — driver must stop immediately
2. Count bean bags in the scoring zone that were properly intaked
3. Record the score
4. Reset the field for the next run or next student

## Strategy Guide

### Route Planning

Before your run, look at the field layout and plan your route:

| Strategy | Description | Best When |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Nearest first** | Drive to the closest bean bag, intake, score, repeat | Bean bags are spread evenly |
| **Sweep line** | Drive in a straight line across the field, intaking everything in your path | Bean bags are clustered in a line |
| **Cluster attack** | Target the area with the most bean bags first | Bean bags are grouped together |
| **Perimeter sweep** | Drive around the edges first, then hit the center | Bean bags are near the walls |

### Driving for Intake Success

1. **Approach straight** — your intake works best when the bean bag enters dead center
2. **Slow down on contact** — driving too fast means the bean bag bounces off instead of being intaked
3. **Confirm the intake** — glance at your robot to verify the bean bag is fully inside before moving to the next one
4. **Score frequently** — do not try to hold 5 bean bags at once. Intake 1-2, score, repeat

### Intake Tuning Reminders

If your intake is not performing well during practice runs:

| Symptom | Likely Cause | Quick Fix |
|---------|-------------|-----------|
| Bean bag slips under the rollers | Compression too low | Lower the intake or add roller wrap |
| Bean bag gets stuck in the intake | Compression too high or deadzone | Raise the intake slightly or add a guide ramp |
| Bean bag bounces off on approach | Driving too fast or intake too slow | Slow down approach, increase roller speed |
| Intake stalls when grabbing | Motor overloaded, too much compression | Reduce compression, check free-spin |
| Bean bag falls out while driving | No retention mechanism | Add a back plate or second roller |

!!! tip "Two Minutes of Tuning Can Win the Challenge"
    Between your two attempts, you get time to adjust. If your first run revealed any issues, fix them immediately. Loosen or tighten compression, adjust roller height, change driving strategy. The students who improve between attempts are the ones who win.

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Impact | Fix |
|---------|--------|-----|
| Not planning a route | Wasted time driving randomly | Scan the field for 10 seconds before starting |
| Trying to intake from the side | Intake only works from the front | Always approach head-on |
| Holding too many bean bags | Robot gets heavy, intake jams | Score after every 1-2 intakes |
| Not practicing between runs | Same mistakes repeated | Use the gap between runs to tune and strategize |
| Panicking at the clock | Sloppy driving, missed intakes | Practice under time pressure before the challenge |
