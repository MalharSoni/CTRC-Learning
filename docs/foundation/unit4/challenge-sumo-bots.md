# Challenge: Sumo Bots

**Last robot standing wins.** Sumo Bots is the ultimate test of your wedge design, drivetrain power, and driving skill. Two robots enter the circular sumo field. One gets pushed off. Simple rules, deep strategy.

---

## Objective

Push your opponent's robot off the circular sumo field. The last robot remaining on the platform wins the round.

---

## Format

| | Details |
|---|---|
| **Type** | 1v1 head-to-head |
| **Matches** | Best of 3 rounds per match |
| **Tournament** | Double elimination bracket |
| **Round Duration** | 60 seconds (if no one is pushed off, judges decide) |
| **Mechanism Required** | Wedge attachment from Module 4.1 |

---

## The Sumo Field

- **Shape**: Circular platform
- **Surface**: Standard VEX field tiles or smooth flat surface
- **Boundary**: Clearly marked edge — any robot that touches the ground beyond the edge is eliminated for that round
- **Starting positions**: Robots start on opposite sides of the circle, facing each other

## Rules

### Before the Match
1. Robots must pass a **safety inspection** before competing:
    - No sharp edges or points that could damage other robots or the field
    - All screws tightened, no loose parts
    - Wedge securely attached
    - Battery secured and wiring managed
2. Robots must use their **wedge attachment from Module 4.1**
3. Robot must fit within standard VEX size constraints (18" x 18" x 18")

### During the Match
1. Coach says **"3, 2, 1, Go!"** — drivers begin
2. **Driving only** — no autonomous period for this challenge
3. **Contact is expected** — pushing, wedging, and shoving are the entire point
4. If a robot is **fully off the platform** (all wheels past the edge), that robot loses the round
5. If a robot is **partially off** (some wheels over the edge) but recovers, play continues
6. If **neither robot is eliminated** after 60 seconds, judges award the round based on aggression and field control

!!! example "📷 Photo Needed"
    Two robots pushing on sumo field, wedges engaged

!!! warning "No Weapon Attachments"
    Sumo is about pushing, not damaging. **Wedges only** — no spinning mechanisms, no launchers, no parts designed to break the opponent's robot. Any robot deemed to have a "weapon" is disqualified. The wedge must be a passive, non-powered attachment.

### Penalties
| Violation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| Intentionally damaging opponent's robot | Disqualification |
| Starting before "Go" | Round restart (first offense), round loss (second offense) |
| Robot falls apart during match | Round loss (rebuild and continue bracket if possible) |
| Unsportsmanlike conduct | Disqualification from tournament |

## Strategy Guide

### The Fundamentals

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|------------|
| **Get under them** | Lower wedge angle wins — you lift their wheels, they lose traction |
| **Control the center** | The robot in the center has more room to maneuver |
| **Face them head-on** | Your wedge only works from the front — never show your side or back |
| **Push toward the edge** | Once you are under them, drive straight toward the nearest edge |

!!! example "📷 Photo Needed"
    One robot's wedge getting under another, lifting opponent's wheels

### Driving Techniques

#### The Approach
- Drive forward at **medium speed** toward your opponent
- Aim your wedge at their front wheels or the gap between their wedge and the ground
- Do not charge at full speed — you will overshoot and lose position

#### The Lock
- Once your wedge is under them, **accelerate to full power**
- Keep driving straight — any turning reduces your pushing force
- Maintain pressure until they are off the edge

#### The Recovery
- If they get under YOU, **reverse immediately** and create distance
- Reset and approach from a different angle
- Try to circle to their side where their wedge is not protecting them

#### The Edge Game
- When near the edge, keep your **center of gravity low and centered**
- Avoid spinning near the edge — momentum can carry you off
- If you are being pushed toward the edge, turn 90 degrees to redirect the push along the edge instead of over it

### Advanced Strategy

!!! tip "Watch Their Wheels"
    When your wedge is under the opponent and their wheels are spinning in the air, they have zero traction. That is your moment to push. If their wheels are still on the ground, your wedge is not low enough — readjust.

### Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Loses |
|---------|-------------|
| Charging at full speed from the start | You overshoot and end up behind them |
| Turning while pushing | Reduces pushing force, lets them escape |
| Getting pushed sideways | Your wedge is only on the front — reposition |
| Ignoring field position | Fighting near your own edge means one mistake ends the round |
| Giving up after losing the wedge battle | Circle and re-engage — one good push can still win |

## Tournament Bracket

### Double Elimination Format

- **Winners bracket**: Win and advance, lose and drop to losers bracket
- **Losers bracket**: Win and keep going, lose and you are eliminated
- **Finals**: Winners bracket champion vs losers bracket champion
- If the losers bracket champion wins the first finals match, a second match is played (true double elimination)

### Seeding

Seeds are determined by:
1. Module 4.1 build quality score (coach assessment)
2. Free-spin test results from Unit 1
3. Random draw for ties
