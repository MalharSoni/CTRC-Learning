# Unit 4: Mechanisms

**The drivetrain moves your robot. Mechanisms make your robot do things.** Unit 4 teaches you to design, CAD, and build real game mechanisms — wedges for pushing, intakes for collecting, and linear sliders for reaching. Every mechanism follows the same process: CAD it first, get coach approval, then build it.

## The Build Process

Every mechanism in Unit 4 follows the same workflow:

```
CAD It → Coach Approval → Build It → Test It → Compete
```

### Why CAD First?

!!! tip "Measure Twice, Cut Never"
    By CADing your mechanism before building, you catch design mistakes when they are free to fix — in the computer. Once you start cutting metal and assembling, mistakes cost time and parts. The 20 minutes you spend in Onshape saves 2 hours of rebuilding.

### Coach Approval Gate

Before you build anything physical, your coach must review and approve your CAD model. They will check for:

- Structural integrity (will it break under load?)
- Correct part selection (are you using the right screws, gears, spacers?)
- Interference (do parts collide when the mechanism moves?)
- Feasibility (can this actually be built with the parts we have?)

Each student or pair will need access to:

- Completed drivetrain from Unit 1

- Flex wheels or rubber rollers (for intake)

- Additional motors (11W or 5.5W)
- Screw joints hardware (shoulder screws, spacers, nylock nuts)
- Various gears (for intake gearing)
- Zipties and rubber bands

---

## Tips for Success

!!! tip "Free-Spin First, Motor Second"
    Before attaching any motor to any mechanism, spin it by hand. If it does not spin freely and smoothly, adding a motor will not fix the problem — it will burn out the motor. Fix the friction first, then add power.

!!! warning "Do Not Skip CAD"
    Students who skip the CAD step and jump straight to building almost always rebuild their mechanism at least once. Students who CAD first almost never do. The data is clear — CAD saves time.
