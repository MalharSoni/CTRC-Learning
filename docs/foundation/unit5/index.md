## How This Unit Is Different

Units 1-4 taught you individual skills in isolation. Unit 5 is about **integration** — making all of those skills work together under pressure.

| Previous Units | Unit 5 |
|---------------|--------|
| Build one thing at a time | Multiple systems must coexist on one robot |
| Test in controlled conditions | Compete against real opponents |
| Work individually | Work as an alliance team |
| Unlimited time | 60-second matches, every second counts |
| One goal at a time | Balance offense, defense, autonomous, and strategy simultaneously |

!!! info "This Is What Competition Feels Like"
    Real VEX tournaments are chaotic. Robots break. Autonomous routines miss. Alliance partners do unexpected things. The teams that succeed are the ones who prepared for chaos — not the ones who only practiced in perfect conditions. Unit 5 is your preparation for chaos.

## Prerequisites

Before starting Unit 5, you must have:

- [x] Completed drivetrain from Unit 1 (functional, reliable)
- [x] Working driver controls from Unit 2 (responsive, all mechanisms mapped)
- [x] At least two mechanisms built and attached (from Unit 4)
- [x] Basic autonomous routine from Unit 2 (drive forward, turn)

If any of these are incomplete, finish them before proceeding. Unit 5 assumes a working robot — there is no time for foundational builds.

---

## Tips for Success

!!! tip "Integration Is Where Most Teams Struggle"
    Building individual mechanisms is the easy part. Making them all work together on one robot without interfering with each other — that is the hard part. Budget extra time for integration. Things that worked perfectly in isolation will fight each other when combined.

!!! tip "Reliability Beats Performance"
    A robot that scores 20 points every match beats a robot that scores 40 points one match and breaks the next two. Focus on making your systems reliable before making them fast or powerful.
