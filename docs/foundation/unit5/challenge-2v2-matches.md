# Challenge: 2v2 Alliance Matches — The CTRC Foundation Championship

**This is it.** Everything you have learned across all five units comes together here. The Foundation Championship is a full tournament experience — alliance selection, autonomous periods, driver control, scouting, strategy, and awards. When you walk off this field, you will know what a real VEX competition feels like.

---

## Objective

Compete in 2v2 alliance matches using the Sack Attack and Ball Sweep game formats from Unit 4. Alliances score points through autonomous routines and driver control, competing in a bracket to determine the Foundation Champion.

---

## Tournament Format

| | Details |
|---|---|
| **Team Size** | 2 robots per alliance (4 robots per match) |
| **Games Played** | Sack Attack and Ball Sweep (alternating or coach's choice) |
| **Match Format** | Autonomous period (15 sec) + Driver control (45 sec) = 60 sec total |
| **Qualification** | Round robin (every alliance plays every other alliance) |
| **Elimination** | Top 4 alliances, single elimination bracket |
| **Final** | Best of 3 |

---

## Alliance Selection Process

### Seeding

Individual students are seeded based on their performance across all previous challenges:

| Factor | Weight | Source |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Sumo Bots placement | 20% | Unit 4 |
| Sack Attack best score | 20% | Unit 4 |
| Ball Sweep record | 20% | Unit 4 |
| CAD challenge scores | 15% | Unit 3 |
| Robot reliability rating | 15% | Coach assessment |
| Autonomous routine score | 10% | Unit 2 / 5.2 |

### Selection Order

1. Students are ranked by their seed score
2. The **#1 seed announces their pick** — any other student
3. That student **accepts or declines** (one decline allowed)
4. The **#2 seed picks**, and so on
5. Process continues until all alliances are formed
6. If there is an odd number of students, the last student joins the lowest-seeded alliance as a third member (rotates driving duties)

!!! info "Alliance Selection Is a Skill"
    This is not random pairing. You have been scouting all through Units 3 and 4. Use that information. Pick a partner whose robot complements yours and whose autonomous does not conflict with yours. This is the most important decision of the tournament.

## Match Structure

!!! example "📷 Photo Needed"
    Full competition field set up for 2v2 matches with game objects placed

### Pre-Match (2 minutes)

1. Alliances meet at the field
2. Partners discuss and confirm:
    - Autonomous selection (which routine each robot runs)
    - Starting positions (left vs right, which side of the field)
    - Match roles (offense vs defense)
    - Communication plan (key callouts)
3. Robots placed in starting positions
4. Drivers ready with controllers

### Autonomous Period (15 seconds)

!!! example "📷 Photo Needed"
    Robots running autonomous — drivers with hands off controllers

1. Coach signals **"Autonomous!"** — autonomous routines begin
2. **Drivers may not touch controllers** during autonomous
3. Both alliances' robots execute their programmed routines simultaneously
4. After 15 seconds, coach signals **"Driver control!"**

### Driver Control Period (45 seconds)

!!! example "📷 Photo Needed"
    2v2 match in progress — all four robots on field

1. Drivers take control of their robots
2. Normal gameplay — score game objects, play defense, execute strategy
3. All mechanisms allowed (intake, slider, wedge)
4. Alliance communication encouraged (callouts, direction)

### End of Match

1. Coach signals **"Match over!"** at 60 seconds
2. All robots stop immediately
3. Scoring is tallied:
    - **Sack Attack**: Count bean bags in each alliance's scoring zone
    - **Ball Sweep**: Count balls on each half of the field

---

## Scoring

### Sack Attack Scoring

| Action | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Bean bag scored during autonomous | **2 points** (double value!) |
| Bean bag scored during driver control | **1 point** |
| Autonomous bonus (alliance that scores more in auto) | **3 points** |

### Ball Sweep Scoring

| Result | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Alliance with fewer balls on their half | **Win** |
| Autonomous bonus: alliance that sweeps more balls in auto | **First ball placement advantage** for driver control |
| Tie at buzzer | **Autonomous winner wins the match** |

## Tournament Schedule

### Phase 1: Qualification Rounds (Round Robin)

Every alliance plays every other alliance at least once. The results determine seeding for elimination.

| Qualification Result | Impact |
|---------------------|--------|
| Win | +2 ranking points |
| Tie | +1 ranking point |
| Loss | 0 ranking points |
| Autonomous bonus win | +1 additional ranking point |

### Phase 2: Elimination Bracket (Top 4)

```
  Semifinal 1: #1 Seed vs #4 Seed
  Semifinal 2: #2 Seed vs #3 Seed

  Final: Winner of SF1 vs Winner of SF2
  (Best of 3)
```

!!! example "📐 Diagram Needed"

### Between Matches

Teams get **3-5 minutes** between matches for:

- Quick repairs (no major rebuilds)
- Strategy discussion with partner
- Autonomous routine adjustments
- Battery swaps

## Rules and Conduct

### Robot Rules

- Robots must pass inspection before the tournament (size, safety, no weapons)
- Robots must be the student's own build from Units 1-4 (no outside robots)
- One robot per student (no sharing or swapping robots between matches)
- Battery must be charged above 50% at match start

### Match Rules

- No intentional damage to opponent robots
- No pinning an opponent for more than 5 seconds (must release)
- No reaching outside the field boundary
- Autonomous routines must be the student's own code
- If a robot disconnects, the match continues — no restarts for disconnections

### Conduct Rules

- **Gracious Professionalism** — respect every opponent, partner, and coach
- No trash talking or unsportsmanlike behavior
- Celebrate wins respectfully, accept losses gracefully
- Help opponents if their robot is stuck (after the match)
- Thank your alliance partner after every match, win or lose

!!! warning "Gracious Professionalism Is Not Optional"
    In VEX Robotics, Gracious Professionalism is a core value. Teams that win with poor sportsmanship are not respected. Teams that lose with grace are. The Foundation Championship enforces this — repeated unsportsmanlike conduct results in disqualification.

## Awards

The Foundation Championship concludes with an awards ceremony recognizing excellence across multiple categories:

| Award | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| **Tournament Champion** | Winning alliance in the elimination bracket |
| **Best Driver** | Voted by peers and coaches — smoothest, most effective driver |
| **Best Autonomous** | Most reliable, highest-scoring autonomous routine |
| **Best Strategy** | Alliance that demonstrated the smartest match strategy |
| **Most Improved** | Student who showed the greatest improvement from Unit 1 to Unit 5 |
| **Gracious Professional** | Student who best exemplified sportsmanship and teamwork |
| **Best Design** | Best overall robot design (CAD quality + build quality + functionality) |

### Award Voting

- **Tournament Champion**: Determined by match results
- **Best Driver, Best Strategy**: Voted by peers (anonymous ballot)
- **Best Autonomous, Best Design**: Selected by coaches
- **Most Improved, Gracious Professional**: Selected by coaches with peer input
