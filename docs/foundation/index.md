# V5 Foundation Program

The **CTRC V5 Foundation Program** is a structured, hands-on curriculum designed to take students from zero robotics experience to competition-ready builders and programmers. Whether you've never touched a screwdriver or you've been tinkering for years, this program meets you where you are and levels you up.

---

## Program Philosophy

### Learn It. Build It. Prove It.

Every unit in the Foundation Program follows the same three-phase cycle:

| Phase | What It Means | How It Works |
|-------|--------------|--------------|
| **Learn It** | Understand the concept | Lectures, demos, visual guides |
| **Build It** | Apply hands-on | Guided builds, activities, experiments |
| **Prove It** | Demonstrate mastery | Field challenges, quizzes, timed tests |

!!! tip "Why This Works"
    Robotics is not a spectator sport. You cannot learn to build robots by watching videos. Every module includes physical activities that force you to apply what you've learned — and every unit ends with a challenge that proves you can do it under pressure.

---

## Program Structure

The Foundation Program is divided into **5 units**, each focusing on a critical domain of competitive robotics. Units build on each other — you must complete them in order.

### Unit Overview

| Unit | Title | Modules | Challenges | Focus |
|------|-------|---------|------------|-------|
| **1** | [Drivetrain Mastery](unit1/index.md) | 4 | 2 | Parts, structure, gear ratios, building a drivetrain |
| **2** | [Programming](unit2/index.md) | 5 | 2 | VSCode, LemLib, controls, PID, autonomous |
| **3** | [Onshape CAD](unit3/index.md) | 5 | 3 | 3D modeling, assemblies, rendering, design iteration |
| **4** | [Mechanisms](unit4/index.md) | 3 | 3 | Wedges, intakes, linear sliders, game mechanisms |
| **5** | [Competition Ready](unit5/index.md) | 3 | 1 | Integration, autonomous, strategy, 2v2 matches |

---

## What You'll Build

By the end of the Foundation Program, every student will have:

- [x] Built a fully functional competition drivetrain from scratch
- [x] Programmed driver controls, button-mapped mechanisms, and autonomous routines
- [x] Designed robot components in Onshape CAD
- [x] Built and integrated at least two game mechanisms
- [x] Competed in simulated match play with strategy and scouting

---

## How Challenges Work

Each unit includes **field challenges** — timed, scored, competitive events that test everything you learned. These are not optional. Challenges are where you prove your skills.

!!! example "Challenge Format"
    - **Individual or team-based** depending on the challenge
    - **Timed** — fastest completion or best score wins
    - **Head-to-head brackets** for elimination rounds
    - **Best of 3 attempts** to allow for iteration
    - **Scored by mentors** with clear rubrics

---

## Prerequisites

**None.** This program assumes zero prior experience. You need:

- Willingness to learn
- Ability to follow safety rules
- Patience with yourself and your teammates

!!! info "For Experienced Students"
    If you already have VEX experience, you can test out of individual modules by passing the module quiz with 90%+ and completing the associated build activity to mentor satisfaction. You still must complete all field challenges.

---

## Getting Started

Start with **[Unit 1: Drivetrain Mastery](unit1/index.md)** — it's the foundation everything else builds on. You can't program a robot you haven't built, and you can't build a robot if you don't know your parts.

Good luck. Build something great.
