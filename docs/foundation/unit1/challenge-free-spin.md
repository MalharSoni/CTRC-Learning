# Challenge: Free Spin Challenge

**The ultimate test of friction management.** This challenge measures one thing: how long your drivetrain wheels spin freely after a single push. Every second of spin time reflects the quality of your bearings, spacing, screw joints, and alignment.

---

## Objective

Achieve the **longest wheel free-spin time** on your completed drivetrain.

---

## Rules

| Rule | Details |
|------|---------|
| **Robot** | Must be your completed drivetrain from Module 1.4 |
| **Motors** | Must be disengaged (remove cartridges or disconnect shafts from wheels) |
| **The Push** | One firm push of the robot across a flat surface (or one firm hand-spin of a wheel) |
| **Timing** | Stopwatch starts when you release the robot/wheel. Stops when all wheels stop spinning. |
| **Attempts** | Best of 3 attempts counts as your score |
| **Modifications** | You may make adjustments between attempts (re-spacing, tightening, etc.) |

!!! warning "No External Lubrication"
    You may not apply oil, grease, WD-40, or any lubricant to your robot. Friction reduction must come from proper building technique only.

---

## Scoring

| Time | Rating | What It Means |
|------|--------|--------------|
| **0–2 seconds** | Needs Work | Significant friction problems — likely missing bearings or over-constrained |
| **2–4 seconds** | Acceptable | Basic friction management but room for major improvement |
| **4–6 seconds** | Good | Solid building technique, properly spaced and bearing-supported |
| **6–8 seconds** | Excellent | High-quality assembly with attention to every friction source |
| **8–10 seconds** | Outstanding | Competition-quality friction management |
| **10+ seconds** | Elite | You understand friction at an advanced level |

---

## How to Win

!!! tip "Friction Reduction Strategies"

    **Bearings everywhere:**
    Every axle must pass through bearings, not bare metal holes. This alone can double your spin time.

    **Screw joints where possible:**
    If a wheel or roller doesn't need to be on a shared axle, consider mounting it on a shoulder screw (screw joint) for lower friction than an axle-in-bearing setup.

    **Spacer every gap:**
    Any rotating component touching a stationary surface creates axial friction. Space everything apart. Even a 1/16" spacer between a gear face and a C-Channel wall makes a measurable difference.

    **Controlled play, not tight packing:**
    Don't crank shaft collars tight against bearings. Leave ~1mm of play so nothing binds.

    **Check alignment:**
    A bent axle or misaligned bearings create friction that no amount of spacing will fix. Sight down each axle — it should spin true without wobble.

    **Gear mesh quality:**
    If your gear train is part of the spinning system, gear mesh distance matters enormously. Too tight and teeth bind. Find the sweet spot where gears turn smoothly with minimal backlash.

## Testing Procedure

### Before Your Attempts

1. Disengage all motors from the drivetrain
2. Place robot on a clean, flat, hard surface (tile or smooth table)
3. Spin each wheel individually by hand — identify any that feel rough or sticky
4. Fix any issues found

!!! example "📷 Photo Needed"
    Robot on flat surface, motors disengaged, ready for free spin test

### During Each Attempt

1. Place robot on the surface
2. Give one firm, even push (not a slam — consistent force)
3. Release and start the stopwatch
4. Watch all wheels — time stops when the **last wheel** stops
5. Record the time

!!! example "🎬 GIF/Video Needed"
    Proper push technique: one firm push, release, wheels continue spinning

### Between Attempts

You have **3 minutes between attempts** to make adjustments:

- Re-space components
- Adjust shaft collar positions
- Check bearing seating
- Realign axles
- Adjust gear mesh distance

!!! example "Diagnosis Guide"
    **If one wheel stops much earlier than the others:**
    That wheel's axle has a friction problem. Check its bearings, spacing, and any components on that specific axle.

    **If all wheels stop at roughly the same time (but too quickly):**
    You likely have a systemic issue — bearings missing throughout, or all spacing is too tight.

    **If wheels spin well individually but stop fast when the robot rolls:**
    Your gear train may be binding, or the frame is flexing under the weight and causing misalignment. Check structural rigidity.

## Bracket Format

After all students complete their 3 attempts, the top scores are ranked:

1. **1st Place** — Longest spin time
2. **2nd Place** — Second longest
3. **3rd Place** — Third longest

Ties are broken by second-best attempt time.

!!! info "This Challenge Repeats"
    You can re-attempt this challenge at any point during the Foundation Program as you improve your building skills. Many students achieve dramatically better times after completing Unit 3 (CAD) and Unit 4 (Mechanisms) because they better understand precision assembly.

## Key Takeaways

- [x] Friction management is a measurable, testable skill — not guesswork
- [x] Bearings, spacers, and screw joints are your primary friction-reduction tools
- [x] The difference between "acceptable" and "elite" is attention to detail
- [x] Iteration between attempts teaches you more than any lecture
- [x] This skill directly translates to competition performance — low-friction robots are faster and more power-efficient
