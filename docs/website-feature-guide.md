# Website Feature Guide

This guide covers the features built into this site to help you get the most out of your learning experience. Take a few minutes to familiarize yourself with these tools before diving into the course content.

---

## Dark Mode Toggle

You can switch between **light mode** and **dark mode** using the toggle in the site header. The site will remember your preference across sessions.

- Click the sun/moon icon in the top navigation bar
- Your preference is saved in your browser's local storage
- All diagrams and images are designed to work in both modes

---

## Search

The site includes a powerful full-text search feature.

- Click the **search icon** or press ++slash++ to open search
- Type any keyword to find relevant pages
- Results update in real-time as you type
- Search covers all page titles, headings, and body content

!!! tip "Quick Search Shortcut"
    Press ++slash++ anywhere on the site to instantly focus the search bar. This is the fastest way to find specific topics.

---

## Navigation

### Sidebar Navigation
The left sidebar shows the full site structure. Click any section to expand it and reveal sub-pages. Your current location is highlighted.

### Table of Contents
On wider screens, the right sidebar shows a table of contents for the current page. Click any heading to jump directly to that section.

### Previous / Next
At the bottom of each page, you'll find navigation links to the previous and next pages in the learning sequence.

---

## Admonition Boxes

Throughout the site, you'll see colored callout boxes that highlight important information. Here are the types you'll encounter:

!!! note "Note"
    Notes provide additional context or background information that supplements the main content. They're helpful but not critical.

!!! tip "Tip"
    Tips offer practical advice, shortcuts, or best practices that can save you time or help you work more effectively.

!!! warning "Warning"
    Warnings alert you to common mistakes, pitfalls, or important considerations you should be aware of before proceeding.

!!! example "Example"
    Examples demonstrate a concept with a concrete illustration or walkthrough. These are especially common in exercise pages.

!!! info "Info"
    Info boxes provide supplementary details, definitions, or clarifications about a topic.

!!! danger "Danger"
    Danger boxes highlight critical mistakes that could cause significant problems — like losing work or breaking assemblies.

---

## Image Lightbox

Most images on this site support a **lightbox** feature:

- **Click any image** to open it in a full-screen overlay
- Use the overlay controls to **zoom in** on details
- Click outside the image or press ++escape++ to close
- This is especially useful for detailed CAD screenshots and diagrams

---

## Code Blocks

Some pages include code-like blocks for formulas, Onshape FeatureScript, or configuration snippets.

```
Gear Ratio = Driven Teeth / Driving Teeth
Example: 60T / 12T = 5:1 reduction
```

Code blocks support syntax highlighting and can be copied to your clipboard with the copy button in the top-right corner.

## Tabs

Some content is organized into tabs to keep the page clean while offering multiple views of the same topic.

=== "Gears"
    Gears are rigid toothed wheels that mesh together to transmit power. They offer precise ratios and high efficiency.

=== "Belts"
    Belts use flexible toothed loops running over pulleys. They're lighter than gears and can span longer distances.

=== "Chain"
    Chain and sprocket systems are similar to belts but use metal chain links. They handle higher loads and resist slipping.
