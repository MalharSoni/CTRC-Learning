# Checkpoint: Mechanical Assembly Complete

You've assembled your robot's mechanical chassis. Before handing it off to your teacher for electronics installation, verify everything is structurally sound and ready for wiring.

---

## What You've Accomplished

- [x] Installed N20 drive motors in chassis pockets
- [x] Attached wheels to motor D-shafts
- [x] Assembled multi-piece chassis (if applicable)
- [x] Mounted weapon motor securely
- [x] Installed weapon with proper clearance
- [x] Added armor panels (if applicable)
- [x] Checked final mechanical weight

---

## Mechanical Assembly Verification Checklist

### Drive System

- [ ] Both N20 motors are securely press-fit or glued in pockets
- [ ] Motors do not wobble or rotate when pulled gently
- [ ] Wheels are attached to motor D-shafts with flats aligned
- [ ] Wheels spin freely when turned by hand (no rubbing on chassis)
- [ ] Wheels have grip (tread pattern, silicone wrap, or texture)
- [ ] Left and right wheels are symmetrical

### Weapon System

- [ ] Weapon motor is rigidly mounted (will not shift under vibration)
- [ ] Weapon is attached to motor shaft via TPU hub or rigid hub
- [ ] Weapon spins freely by hand with no wobbling
- [ ] Weapon has **2mm minimum clearance** from all chassis parts when spinning
- [ ] Weapon balance is correct (spins smoothly, doesn't vibrate)
- [ ] Weapon lock mechanism is functional (pin, clip, or bracket prevents weapon rotation)

### Structural Integrity

- [ ] All screws are tight (but not over-tightened)
- [ ] No cracks in 3D printed parts
- [ ] Chassis feels rigid (doesn't flex excessively when squeezed)
- [ ] Battery compartment is sized correctly with retention method
- [ ] Malenki Nano mount is secure and positioned away from weapon

### Weight Verification

- [ ] Total mechanical weight (chassis + motors + wheels + weapon) is 400-520g
- [ ] Robot is not significantly overweight (allows 80-100g for electronics, battery, wires)
- [ ] Robot is not significantly underweight (if under 450g, consider adding armor)

### Functional Tests

- [ ] Spin each wheel by hand — both should rotate smoothly
- [ ] Spin weapon by hand — should rotate freely without hitting anything
- [ ] Flex chassis gently — should feel solid, not fragile
- [ ] Remove and reinstall weapon — should be easy to do (for repairs between fights)

## Pre-Handoff Preparation

### Photo Documentation

Take clear photos of your robot from multiple angles:

1. **Top view** — showing overall layout
2. **Front view** — showing weapon and wedge (if applicable)
3. **Side views (left and right)** — showing motor mounts and clearances
4. **Bottom view** — showing battery compartment and Nano mount
5. **Close-ups** — motor pockets, weapon mount, wheel attachment

These photos serve as your assembly reference for repairs during competition.

### Labeling

Mark components that might need identification later:

- **"L"** and **"R"** on left and right motor pockets (if not obvious)
- **"TOP"** on chassis (if orientation could be confusing)
- **"FRONT"** arrow pointing toward weapon
- Your name or initials on the underside

### What to Bring to Teacher Handoff

1. **Completed mechanical chassis**
2. **Weapon lock** (installed to show it works)
3. **Assembly photos** (printed or digital)
4. **Spare parts** (if any): extra screws, spare wheel, etc.
5. **Your Onshape design** (link or access) in case dimensions need checking

## Teacher Handoff Notes

### What Your Teacher Will Add

**Electronics:**
- Malenki Nano control board
- 2x N20 motor wires (soldered to Nano)
- Brushless weapon motor wires (3-phase, soldered to Nano)
- LiPo battery
- Battery connector
- Power switch (if using separate switch)

**Wiring:**
- All solder joints with heat shrink
- Wire routing away from weapon path
- Strain relief at Nano connections
- Labeled wires (positive/negative, motor A/B, weapon)

**Safety Setup:**
- Transmitter binding
- Failsafe configuration
- Power-on/power-off procedure verification

**Testing:**
- Drive motor direction check
- Weapon motor spin direction check
- Failsafe test (critical safety check)
- Final weight verification

## What Happens Next

**Phase 8 (Teacher):** [Malenki Nano Setup](malenki-nano-setup.md)

Your teacher will:

1. Wire the Malenki Nano to battery, drive motors, and weapon motor
2. Bind the Nano to a radio transmitter
3. Configure failsafe (weapon stops if signal lost)
4. Test all systems (drive, weapon, failsafe)
5. Route and secure all wires
6. Take photos of completed wiring for repair reference

**Estimated time:** 1-2 hours per robot

**Phase 9 (Teacher):** [Testing & Safety](testing-and-safety.md)

Your teacher will:

1. Verify LiPo battery safety
2. Execute power-on/power-off sequences
3. Test drive systems (forward, reverse, turning)
4. Test weapon system (in arena or test box only)
5. Conduct failsafe test before competition
6. Complete pre-fight checklist
7. Brief you on robot controls and safety procedures

---

## Common Issues Found During Handoff

If your teacher identifies issues, these are fixable:

| Issue | Fix |
|-------|-----|
| Wheel slips on shaft | Add drop of CA glue or wrap shaft with tape before reinstalling |
| Motor falls out of pocket | Add CA glue around motor body perimeter |
| Weapon wobbles | Check hub attachment, verify weapon is balanced, check for bent shaft |
| Chassis cracks | Reinforce with CA glue, add external brace, or reprint part |
| Overweight | Remove non-essential features, reduce infill (reprint lighter), thin armor |
| Weapon hits chassis | Adjust weapon position, file down interference points, or reprint chassis |
