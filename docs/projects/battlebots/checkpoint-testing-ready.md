## Safety Testing Checklist

Your teacher will verify all safety systems before approving your robot for competition:

### LiPo Battery Safety

- [ ] Battery is healthy (no puffing, dents, or damage)
- [ ] Battery voltage is correct (4.2V per cell when fully charged)
- [ ] Battery connector polarity is correct (reversed polarity destroys electronics)
- [ ] Battery is secured in compartment (won't shift during impacts)
- [ ] Battery can be quickly removed for charging

### Power-On/Power-Off Procedure

**Power-On Sequence:**
1. Transmitter on first
2. Weapon lock installed
3. Robot power on
4. Wait for bind confirmation
5. Verify drive control
6. Robot placed in arena
7. Weapon lock removed (only when arena is sealed)

**Power-Off Sequence:**
1. Weapon lock installed first (weapon cannot spin)
2. Robot power off
3. Transmitter off last
4. Remove robot from arena

- [ ] Power-on sequence executed correctly
- [ ] Power-off sequence executed correctly
- [ ] Weapon lock is functional and easy to install/remove

### Drive System Testing

- [ ] Forward drive works (both motors spin forward)
- [ ] Reverse drive works (both motors spin backward)
- [ ] Left turn works (left motor slower/reverse, right motor forward)
- [ ] Right turn works (right motor slower/reverse, left motor forward)
- [ ] Drive direction is correct (weapon-first is forward)
- [ ] No motor stuttering or strange noises

### Weapon System Testing (In Arena/Test Box Only)

- [ ] Weapon spins in correct direction (verify with teacher)
- [ ] Weapon spins up smoothly (no catching or vibration)
- [ ] Weapon spins down when throttle released
- [ ] No excessive vibration (balanced weapon)
- [ ] No unusual motor noises (grinding, whining, clicking)
- [ ] Weapon does not contact chassis during spinning

### Failsafe Testing (Critical Safety Check)

**Failsafe Test Procedure:**

1. Power on robot (in arena or test box, weapon lock removed)
2. Spin weapon to ~30% throttle
3. **Turn off transmitter**
4. Observe: Weapon must spin down and stop
5. Observe: Drive motors must not move
6. If anything keeps running: **ROBOT IS UNSAFE — DO NOT USE**

- [ ] Failsafe test passed (all motors stop when transmitter off)
- [ ] Failsafe is verified before every competition event

### Final Weight Check

- [ ] Total weight with battery is at or near 500g target
- [ ] Robot is not significantly overweight (>550g requires trimming)
- [ ] Robot is competition-legal

## Competition Format

### Match Structure

- **Duration:** 2 minutes
- **Format:** Head-to-head, single elimination or round robin
- **Win conditions:** Knockout (opponent can't drive) or Judges' Decision

### Judging Criteria (If Match Goes Full Time)

| Criteria | Points | What It Means |
|----------|--------|---------------|
| **Damage** | 5 points | Visible damage to opponent (cracks, removed parts, disabled systems) |
| **Aggression** | 3 points | Attacking opponent, initiating contact, weapon-first driving |
| **Control** | 3 points | Dictating the fight, positioning, pushing, strategic driving |

**Strategy:** Hit hard, hit often, don't get hit. Damage scores the most points.

### Knockout Rules

If you cannot show **controlled translational movement** (driving forward, backward, or turning), the referee begins a 10-second countdown. If you can't move by the end of the count, you lose by KO.

- Spinning in place (stuck motor) = KO
- Flipped over but still driving = NOT a KO
- Weapon stopped but drive working = NOT a KO

## Repair Strategy

**Between Fights (Usually 5-10 Minutes):**

You will likely need to repair something. Common repairs:

| Damage | Repair | Time |
|--------|--------|------|
| Cracked chassis | CA glue reinforcement | 2 min |
| Wheel slipping | Add CA glue to shaft | 3 min |
| Motor dead | Swap spare N20 motor | 5 min |
| Weapon broken | Replace with spare weapon | 3 min |
| Wires loose | Re-solder connection | 5 min |
| Armor panels detached | Re-glue or zip tie | 2 min |

**This is why you:**
- Print spare weapons before competition
- Bring spare N20 motors
- Have CA glue and zip ties ready
- Take assembly photos
